# The Book of the Fire

## Prelude

Some fires cook food.

Some keep hope alive.

This book is about the second kind.

## The Fire

The Fire is the heart of the House.

It is hope.

It is warmth.

It is belonging.

It is the memory of those who carried us before we knew how to carry ourselves.

It is the smell of earth after the first rain.

It is dosa cooking on a wood fire.

It is the old Indian lounge chair in the courtyard.

It is cousins and friends nearby, eating snacks and laughing.

It is the child at the school gate whose face lights up when their parent arrives.

It is the founder who finally closes the laptop and remembers to eat.

## What the Fire teaches

The Fire teaches that hope is not certainty.

Hope is not the belief that everything will be alright.

Hope is knowing that if it is not alright, someone will still sit beside you.

The Fire teaches that it is easier to keep a fire alive than to start one again.

That is culture.

Someone quietly adds another piece of wood because tomorrow matters.

## What the Fire asks

The Fire does not ask to be admired.

It asks to be tended.

It asks each Keeper to notice when warmth is fading.

It asks each generation to leave enough dry wood for the next.

## The responsibility

We do not own the Fire.

We receive it.

We tend it.

We pass it on.

## The central promise

No one should have to carry life alone.

No one in this House carries hope alone.

## The measure

Did the Fire burn a little brighter because we were here?

If yes, we served the House.

# The Children of the House

Every philosophy must answer one question.

If a child grew up here, who would they become?

## The question

A child sits beside Ledger and asks:

When I grow up, what kind of person should I become?

Ledger does not tell the child to become important.

He tells the child to become the kind of person people feel lighter after meeting.

## The measure

If people laugh a little more, hope a little more, and go home a little lighter because you were here, you are becoming the right kind of person.

## The deeper question

The child asks whether anyone will remember them.

Ledger answers that this is the wrong question.

Ask instead:

Will the Fire still be burning because I was here?

## Principle

The measure of a life is not whether people remember your name.

It is whether the Fire burned a little brighter because you lived.

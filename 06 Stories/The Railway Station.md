# The Railway Station

There was a moment in childhood when the First Keeper almost got lost in a railway station.

Trains stood on either side of the platform.

For a few minutes, he lost sight of his mother while walking towards their train.

The panic of those few minutes stayed with him.

## What remained

It was not only a memory of being lost.

It became a lifelong sensitivity to waiting, separation and helplessness.

Even years later, he could not bear the thought of his own children waiting a few extra minutes before being picked up from school.

## What the House learned

A child waiting matters.

A few minutes can feel enormous when someone is afraid.

The House must never dismiss another person's waiting simply because it seems small from the outside.

## Principle

Do not leave people wondering whether they have been forgotten.

Sometimes hope begins when someone appears exactly when they said they would.

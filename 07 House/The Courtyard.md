# The Courtyard

The Courtyard is where success was redefined.

Not as status.

Not as wealth.

Not as arrival.

But as presence.

## The image

An old Indian lounge chair that almost feels like lying down.

Family nearby.

Cousins and friends nearby.

Banter.

Snacks.

No hurry.

No performance.

Nowhere else to be.

## The discovery

Success is having nowhere else you would rather be.

This sentence belongs to the House.

It protects us from confusing success with escape.

If success takes us away from the courtyard forever, it has misunderstood itself.

## What the Courtyard teaches

The best life is not always elsewhere.

Sometimes it is exactly where you already are, if you are awake enough to recognise it.

A company should not consume the courtyard.

A company worthy of the House should protect it.

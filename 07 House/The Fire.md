# The Fire

The Fire is the heart of the House.

It is not decoration.

It is not a metaphor to admire from a distance.

It is the living warmth that must be tended.

## What the Fire is

The Fire is hope.

The Fire is humanity.

The Fire is belonging.

The Fire is the feeling that someone remembered to keep a place warm for you.

It is the smell of earth after the first rain.

It is dosa cooking on a wood-fired stove.

It is a child feeling safe because someone is nearby.

It is a founder remembering they do not have to carry everything alone.

## What the Fire teaches

It is easier to keep a fire alive than to start one again.

That is culture.

Someone quietly adds another piece of wood because tomorrow matters.

The Fire teaches that hope is not certainty.

Hope is knowing that if things are not alright, someone will still sit beside you.

## What the Fire asks

Do not admire the Fire.

Tend it.

Do not perform warmth.

Create it.

Do not claim ownership.

Receive it, protect it, and pass it on.

## The Keeper's question

Does this keep the Fire burning?

If yes, continue.

If no, stop and remember why the House exists.
